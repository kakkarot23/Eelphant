People also ask

Do elephants lay eggs in real life?
No. Elephants are placental mammals and give birth to live young after a gestation period of approximately 22 months — the longest of any land animal. They do not lay eggs.
What animal lays the largest eggs?
The ostrich lays the largest eggs of any living bird, weighing up to 1.4 kg. The extinct Elephant Bird (Aepyornis) of Madagascar held the record for the largest bird eggs ever, weighing up to 10 kg.
Are there mammals that lay eggs?
Yes! Monotremes are egg-laying mammals. They include the platypus and four species of echidna. These are the only mammals in the world that lay eggs.
How long is an elephant pregnant?
Elephant pregnancies last approximately 22 months (about 645 days), the longest gestation period of any land mammal. A newborn calf can weigh around 100–120 kg at birth.
